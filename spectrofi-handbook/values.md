# Share openly, solve together

We make a system that enables to kill more people in a defence use case. The technology itself is neither good nor bad; rather, it is the humans using the tech who choose how it is used. It is our responsibility to ensure that products are not sold to hostile actors who oppose Western values. 

There is trend of making [Open Startups](https://open-startup.org), yeah we are not open startup. Our commitment to transparency does not mean that our codebase, machine learning models, internal policies, and metrics are publicly available, for anybody.

Even though we sell our most advanced models exclusively to the public sector, technology leaks. It's only a matter of time before burglars are ransacking empty apartments

Every technology has a shadow side, and ours is no exception. Someone, someday, could plug our tech into an autonomous humanoid that decides we need to get rid of humans. Could we prevent that? Maybe. Can we predict every downstream use case? Absolutely not. No startup can.

Transparency for us means that we are open about the questions of ethicity related to our technology. We want to spark a discussion about the ethics of our technology, and it is our responsibility to draw the line on what is ethically right.
